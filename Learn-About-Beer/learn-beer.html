Lager

One of the most popular and widely available styles, Lagers are known for their crisp and light taste. In general most Lager style beers are not too hoppy and can pair with most foods. Lagers can range from light to dark. Some varieties of Lagers include: Pilsners, Ambers and Dark lagers.

Stout

A dark style beer, Stouts typically have a bold coffee like flavor. They can also have roasty or chocolatey notes. Stouts are also known for their rich creamy head when poured.

India Pale Ale (IPA)

Emerging as one of the most popular craft beers, you will find an IPA in nearly every craft brewery and bar. IPAs tend to be a hoppy and bitter style that usually have higher alcohol content. While typically a very strong bitter variety, some IPAs can be lighter and more citrusy with fruity notes.

Sour Beer

Sour beer has seen a rise in popularity in the last few years. Known for their tart and acidic taste, Sour beer typically gets its flavor from added lactobacillus and Pediococcus during the brewing process. Sour beer varieties to look for include: Belgian-Lambic, Berliner Weisse and Flanders Red.

Hefeweizen

German for "Yeast-Wheat", Hefeweizen is a wheat beer that's smooth and refreshing. This beer has fruity notes and low bitterness. A well rounded and easy drinking beer for most occasions.
